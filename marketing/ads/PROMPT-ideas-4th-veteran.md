# 4th ad — veteran realtor persona (55) — concept brainstorm

**Persona block (create once in Higgsfield characters):**
DIANE — Vistalia veteran-realtor persona: woman in her mid-50s, polished
silver-streaked blonde bob, warm smile with natural laugh lines, tasteful
cream blazer over a silk blouse, delicate gold necklace, real-estate
professional polish, relaxed confident energy. Facial features clear and
undistorted, consistent clothing throughout.

Casting logic: median US realtor is a woman in her mid-50s — this persona is
the audience seeing themselves. Jess covers the young-agent read; Diane covers
the veteran majority.

Claim rules (all scripts below comply): no minutes counts, no $39. Allowed:
"same day / same hour," "first one's free," "narrated in your voice."

---

## Concept 1 — "Experience wins again" (competitive pride) ★ RECOMMENDED

Angle: identity/pride — the veteran quietly out-marketing the twenty-somethings.
Nobody in the current set owns this emotion. Highly shareable among the 45-65
female agent cohort; punches young, so it's safe and funny.

Script: "The twenty-somethings at my brokerage post Reels all day. Cute.
My listings hit the MLS with a full cinematic tour — narrated, music,
the same afternoon. And I have never once opened an editing app.
Experience wins again."

Setting: her tidy home office or staged kitchen, afternoon light, coffee cup.
Delivery: dry, amused, one eyebrow. Zero desperation — pure quiet confidence.

## Concept 2 — "Who's your videographer?" (humble-brag referral)

Angle: social proof with a wink — closest cousin to "secret" but warm pride
instead of playful secrecy.

Script: "A seller asked me who my videographer was. I didn't have the heart
to tell her it's the same listing photos she saw on the MLS. Every listing
I take gets a narrated cinematic tour now — same day. And my last three
listings came from people who saw those videos."

Setting: patio café table, late morning, phone face-down beside her coffee.
Delivery: leans in on the confession, laughs at herself once.

## Concept 3 — "The listing appointment" (win-listings)

Angle: the deepest agent pain — winning the listing, not marketing it.
Veteran + listing presentations = credible pairing.

Script: "Thirty years of listing appointments, and this is the best closer
I've ever had. I show sellers what their home will look like as a cinematic,
narrated tour before they sign. They stop interviewing other agents."

Setting: parked outside a brick colonial, blazer on, folder on passenger seat.
Delivery: matter-of-fact, like advice to a younger colleague.
Note: keep mechanics vague ("show them what it will look like") — honest as a
dramatization, don't imply a specific signed client.

## Concept 4 — "My sellers expect more" (luxury seller-expectation)

Angle: high-end market — sellers demand magazine treatment; pairs with the
Cinematic Luxury showcase style. Narrower audience; good later rotation.

Script: "At my price point, sellers expect magazine coverage. Every listing
gets a cinematic narrated film before the first showing. They assume I have
a production team. It's their own photos."

Setting: standing in a marble foyer or beside a staged console table, evening.
Delivery: unhurried, slightly amused on the last line.
