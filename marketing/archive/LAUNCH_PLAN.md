# EstateMotion — Marketing & Launch Plan

June 2026. Founder-led launch. Budget assumption: ~$0 paid spend until organic signal proves the funnel.

## Positioning

One hook, repeated everywhere: **"Built for MLS compliance."** NOT "never hallucinates" — the model can still warp a cabinet and absolute claims are a false-advertising risk. We sell mechanisms, not promises: fidelity guardrails on every scene, risky rooms (kitchens, baths, pools) shot conservative by design, free per-scene regeneration when something's off, credit refund when a render fails. The occasional bad scene becomes a handled case instead of a broken promise. Secondary hook: speed + price — 25 cinematic videos a month for less than one human-edited video from BoxBrownie.

The trial IS the ad: "Upload your listing photos. Get one cinematic video free. Three minutes." Every piece of content ends with that line.

## Phase 0 — Proof assets (this week, before any announcement)

- Render 5 showcase videos from real listings across property types (entry-level, luxury, pool home, investor flip, rental). Use your own + ask 2-3 agent friends for photo sets.
- Cut 3 side-by-side comparisons: static MLS photos vs the EstateMotion video of the same listing. This is the single highest-converting asset format.
- Fix the landing page: new pricing ($99/$249/$499), "1 free video" trial, MLS-safety section. Remove all Runway/Quick Reel references.
- Set Plausible goals: trial signup, first render, checkout. You cannot evaluate Phase 1 without these.

## Phase 1 — Soft launch to your sphere (weeks 1–2)

Goal: 15–25 trial users you can watch closely. NOT scale — signal.

- Use it on your own active listings first. Post the videos on your real channels as an agent, not as a founder. When other agents ask "who made that?" — that's the funnel working.
- Your office/brokerage: offer the first month free to 5 agents in exchange for feedback + permission to use their videos as showcases.
- Direct outreach to your warm agent network (your CRM is full of them): personal message + their own listing rendered as the demo. Rendering THEIR listing unprompted converts far better than a generic demo. (~$6 each — budget $100 for 15 personalized demos.)
- Watch every render in the audit log. Every failure or ugly scene at this stage is gold — fix before Phase 2.

## Phase 2 — Public launch (weeks 3–6)

- Content engine: the product manufactures its own marketing. 3 posts/week on Instagram Reels + TikTok (agents live there): before/afters, "watch AI direct this $2M listing," MLS-compliance explainers. Vertical 9:16 — which is literally your output format.
- Communities: Lab Coat Agents + local agent Facebook groups (give value, don't spam — post the side-by-sides), BiggerPockets for the investor content mode, real-estate-tech newsletters (HousingWire tech, Inman — pitch the "AI you can trust on MLS" angle; the AI-slop backlash makes this timely).
- Photographer partnerships: listing photographers already own the agent relationship and the photos. Offer 20% recurring referral. One productive photographer = a steady trial stream.
- Skip: Product Hunt (agents aren't there), paid ads (not yet), cold email at scale (your warm network is better and CAN-SPAM is a minefield).

## Phase 3 — Paid acquisition (only after Phase 2 shows ≥10% trial→paid)

Meta ads targeting licensed agents, creative = your best-performing organic side-by-side. Start $50/day. If trial→paid is below 10% organically, paid will only lose money faster — fix conversion first.

## Targets

- Phase 1: 20 trials, ≥5 paid conversions (validates ≥25% warm conversion)
- Phase 2: 100 trials, ≥12 paid (≥12% cold conversion), MRR ≥ $2,500
- Watch: time-to-first-render (should be <10 min from signup), render failure rate (<5%), trial CAC at ~$6/video COGS

## Capacity guardrail

The worker is a single Render instance — ~3-4 concurrent renders. Phase 1-2 volume is fine; if a post goes viral, renders queue rather than fail, but watch fal.ai balance (set an auto-recharge + alert) and upgrade the Render instance before any paid push.
